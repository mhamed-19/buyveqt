On mobile the matrix transposes — rows become weekdays Mon–Sun (weekend rows render empty cells, which preserves the calendar metaphor without sacrificing density). Legend collapses to a single inline gradient strip below the grid. Hover becomes tap, tooltip pins below the tapped cell with a 3-second auto-dismiss. The whole component is ~280px tall, fits one mobile fold.

  • Hover/tap a cell → tooltip with date, % change, severity zone
  • Click a cell → deep-links to /weekly/‹week› (if a dispatch was filed) or to /learn/veqt-is-down for unusual/rare days
  • Today's cell always carries the vermilion ring — even when the hover is elsewhere
  • Empty cells (holidays, weekends on desktop) are dashed, not solid — reads as "no print today"

№ 04

The archive,
given a spine.

Twenty-five articles is too many for a "three to start with" home block and too few for a search-and-filter index. The right answer is the one your taxonomy already implies: a syllabus on top, a publishing cadence in the middle, and a sectioned archive below. A new reader can start; a returning reader can graze; a regular reader has something new to expect each week.

A. The cadence (proposed)

Stop publishing in batches. Move to a fixed weekly rhythm with named slots. The slot tells the reader what kind of thing they're getting before they click. Five slots, four weeks; one slot is held for whenever-the-market-requires-it.

DaySlotWhat it isExisting fits
Mon The LookbackOne number, with context. 500-word stat-of-the-week. "VEQT has had 73 down days >1% since inception. Here’s what came after." NEW format
Wed The DispatchThe deep article. Your existing long-form articles. 1500–3000 words, MDX, interactive widgets where they earn it. 22 of 25 fit here
Fri LettersFrom the subreddit, edited. One question lifted from r/CanadianInvestor or r/PersonalFinanceCanada, answered in 300 words with a House Verdict box. NEW format
Sun The WeeklyWhat the market did. Your existing /weekly dispatch — market recap with severity context, region attribution, and a "what to do this week" recommendation. /weekly already exists
Ad-hoc The SpecialWhenever the market requires it. Triggered by a Rare-zone day or a structural news event (MER change, fund split). Short, opinionated, dated within the hour. 3 of 25 fit here

C. Yes — the cadence will help. Specifically, this much

For readers

A reader who lands on the site for the first time on a Wednesday gets The Dispatch — a meaty piece. A reader who lands on a Friday gets Letters — a quick, conversational read. They don’t bounce because the depth doesn’t match the moment they showed up.

A returning reader develops habit. "It's Wednesday, the new dispatch is up." That's the entire newsletter business model in one sentence, and you already have the brand to support it.

For the writer

Five named slots make blank-page paralysis impossible. Monday's prompt is find one number from the data, write 500 words around it. Friday's is scroll the subreddit, find one question, answer it. The Dispatch slot is where you put the energy you used to spread thin across all 25 articles.

It also gives you a way to recycle. A great Dispatch from six months ago becomes Monday’s Lookback this week, with one new chart. The archive starts working for you.
