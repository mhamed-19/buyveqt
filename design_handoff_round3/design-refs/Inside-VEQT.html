The four ETFs that make VEQT.

VEQT holds nothing directly. It owns four other Vanguard funds — one per region — in fixed weights. Today’s move is the weight-blended sum of theirs.

Three things we’re doing differently than XEQTBrief

01 · Lead with contribution, not return

XEQTBrief headlines each region’s daily return. We headline the return and the contribution to VEQT in basis points. Emerging Markets +1.66% sounds like the day’s mover — until you remember EM is 5% of the fund, contributing +0.08pp. The U.S. up 0.51% on a 44% weight contributes +0.22pp. Our region card surfaces both numbers and visually weights the contribution bar.

02 · Directional bars on a center axis

XEQTBrief’s sector bars all start from the left edge, with positives tinted green and negatives tinted red. Reading "is this up or down?" requires colour discrimination at a glance. We center the axis — bars grow left for losses, right for gains — and the number sits adjacent to the bar end. Direction is shape, not just colour.

03 · The leader gets a vermilion cap

The single biggest contributor — the region that moved VEQT most today — gets a 4px stamp-coloured cap above its card. Same treatment in the sector drilldown: the leading sector’s bar is filled in stamp red. A reader scanning the page sees, in one glance: U.S. tech weakness drove the day.
